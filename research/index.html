Asikul Islam

Department of Mathematics, University of Houston

Research Interests

My research centers on the development of efficient and accurate numerical methods for high-dimensional parametric systems governed by partial differential equations. I am broadly interested in numerical analysis, scientific computing, optimization, low-rank approximations, and data-informed modeling.

  • Reduced-order modeling for parametric PDEs
  • Tensor methods and low-rank decompositions
  • PDE-constrained optimization
  • Finite difference and finite volume discretization
  • Operator splitting and time integration methods
  • Scientific computing for biomedical modeling
  • Scientific software development in MATLAB and Python

Current Research

My current research focuses on reduced-order models for coupled reaction-diffusion systems arising in multispecies brain tumor growth simulation. This work combines mathematical modeling, numerical time stepping, tensor-based decompositions, surrogate modeling, and computational performance analysis.

I am also working on numerical methods for PDE-constrained optimization, with emphasis on parabolic PDEs, computational efficiency, and the interface between classical numerical methods and data-driven approximation.

Selected Topics

Reduced-order models for coupled PDE systems

I study projection-based and tensorial surrogate models for parameterized PDE systems, including POD-based methods and tensor-structured reduced-order approximations.

Computational brain tumor modeling

A major application area of my research is glioblastoma growth modeling. I develop numerical models and efficient computational workflows for predicting tumor evolution using coupled reaction-diffusion systems.

Scientific computing and software

I implement and analyze numerical methods using MATLAB, Python, and FEniCSx, with an interest in reliable, reproducible, and scalable computational tools for scientific applications.
