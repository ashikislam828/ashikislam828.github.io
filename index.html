About

A profile that speaks to both academic and industry audiences.

Academic Profile

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in Applied Mathematics at the University of Houston. My research centers on the development of efficient numerical methods for PDE-constrained problems, surrogate modeling, reduced-order modeling, and computational simulation for complex dynamical systems.

I have worked on mathematical models for glioblastoma multi-species tumor growth, numerical time integration, operator splitting, finite difference and finite volume methods, and computational strategies that improve the efficiency and interpretability of large-scale simulations.

Industry-Oriented Profile

Beyond academic research, I bring strengths in mathematical modeling, quantitative analysis, simulation, scientific programming, debugging, visualization, and translating complex technical problems into structured computational workflows. I am interested in opportunities where rigorous modeling and data-informed methods support decision-making, product development, engineering analysis, or research and development.

Research & Selected Work

Core research themes and a few visible highlights.

Reduced-Order Modeling for Parametric PDEs

Developing efficient surrogate models for high-dimensional parametric systems governed by partial differential equations, with emphasis on computational speed, numerical accuracy, and scalability for repeated-query settings.

Computational Brain Tumor Growth Modeling

Designed and implemented numerical models for multispecies glioblastoma growth, integrating advanced mathematical modeling, time-stepping methods, decomposition-based reduction techniques, and computational performance analysis.

Scientific Computing & Solver Development

Working with MATLAB, Python, and FEniCSx to build, test, and compare numerical schemes for PDE simulations, including operator splitting methods, linear algebra routines, and data-driven computational workflows.
