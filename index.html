Asikul Islam

Department of Mathematics, University of Houston

I am a Ph.D. student in Applied Mathematics at the University of Houston. My research interests lie in numerical analysis, scientific computing, reduced-order modeling, optimization, and computational methods for partial differential equations, with applications to biomedical and data-driven modeling.

My current work focuses on efficient surrogate and reduced-order methods for high-dimensional parametric PDE systems, including coupled reaction-diffusion models arising in brain tumor growth simulation. I am interested in rigorous mathematical modeling, scalable computational algorithms, and the translation of advanced numerical methods into practical scientific and industrial workflows.

Brief Summary

My work combines numerical PDEs, reduced-order modeling, tensor methods, scientific programming, and computational simulation. I have developed and studied numerical methods for parabolic PDEs, surrogate models for complex operators, and reduced-order frameworks for reaction-diffusion systems, with applications to biomedical modeling and scientific data analysis.

In addition to research, I have substantial teaching experience in statistics, calculus, numerical analysis, and related mathematics courses, both as a Graduate Teaching Assistant at the University of Houston and as a Lecturer in Bangladesh.
